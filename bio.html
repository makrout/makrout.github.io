Short bio

I was born and raised in Tunisia up to the age of 18. I received my B.E. degree in computer engineering from Ecole Polytechnique de Montreal, Montreal, Canada, and the “Diplome d’Ingenieur” degree from Telecom ParisTech (ENST), Paris, France, both in 2016. I also received the M.S. degree in artificial intelligence from University of Toronto, Toronto, Canada, in 2018. I have worked in the data science industry for 5 years after which I co-founded AIP Labs where I am currently leading its AI development team. I am also a research associate at the Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) department at the University of Manitoba, Winnipeg, Canada.

Research Interests

My research interests lie broadly in optimization, both classical and deep signal processing and antenna theory for wireless communication. At this time, I focus on developing methods and algorithms for:
  • scaling communication systems in terms of the number of antennas, the operational bandwidth and space limitations,
  • combining Shannon and Maxwell theories using physically consistent models,
  • applying self-supervision and active learning for healthcare applications,
  • devising message passing algorithms for a variety of estimation problems,
  • antenna-size constrained channel capacity.
